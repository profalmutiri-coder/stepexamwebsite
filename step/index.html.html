تدريب — القطعة الأولى: Personalities Type A & B

⏱ ص ٣١-٣٢▼
Psychologists have categorized human personalities into two main types: Type A and Type B. People with Type A personalities are typically competitive, highly driven, and intensely organized. They often feel a continuous sense of time urgency, find it extremely difficult to relax, and tend to take on more responsibilities than they can handle. They may become easily stressed or impatient when things do not move at their expected pace. On the other hand, Type B personalities are generally more relaxed, easygoing, and less stressed. They do not become anxious or hostile if they do not win or finish first. They are reflective, highly creative, and allow themselves time to explore without feeling guilt.
Q1: Which trait is most associated with Type A personalities?

  • C
    Preferring to work without any schedule
  • D
    Avoiding organization
💡 B — "a continuous sense of time urgency"
Q2: The word "hostile" most nearly means:
  • A
    Friendly and welcoming
  • B
    Aggressive or unfriendly
  • C
    Extremely creative
  • D
    Indifferent
💡 B — hostile = عدواني / معادٍ
Q3: The underlined "They" refers to:
  • A

تدريب — القطعة الثانية: Thomas Edison

⏱ ص ٣٣-٣٥▼
Thomas Edison was one of the most prolific and famous American inventors in history. Born in 1847, he developed numerous groundbreaking devices in fields such as electric power generation, mass communication, and sound recording. His most famous invention was the practical electric light bulb, which transformed modern industrial society by allowing factories and individuals to work long after dark. Edison was renowned for his incredible work ethic; he famously stated that "genius is one percent inspiration and ninety-nine percent perspiration." Over his lifetime, he held more than 1,000 patents globally.

  • A

    Slowly damaged

  • D

تدريب — القطعة الثالثة: The Invention of Radio

⏱ ص ٣٦-٣٨▼
The invention of the radio revolutionized how information and entertainment were shared across the globe. Before the widespread adoption of the radio, individuals relied primarily on printed newspapers, which could take days or even weeks to deliver breaking news from distant locations. In the early 20th century, wireless telegraphy developed into mass broadcasting, allowing live voices, news updates, and music to be transmitted over immense distances simultaneously. Entire families would gather around radio sets to listen to global updates, sports events, and audio dramas. This shared experience created a unified national culture by connecting remote rural communities with major urban centers.
Q1: Before the radio, how did most people get their news?
  • A
    Through live television
  • B
    From printed newspapers
  • C
    By traveling to urban centers
  • D
    Via wireless telegraph at home
💡 B — "relied primarily on printed newspapers"
Q2: The word "simultaneously" means events occur:
  • A
    One after another with delays
  • B
    At the exact same point in time
  • C
    Only during evening hours
  • D
    In an unorganized manner
💡 B — simultaneously = في نفس الوقت / بالتزامن
Q3: The radio helped build a unified culture by:
  • A
    Encouraging people to buy more newspapers
  • B
    Connecting remote rural areas with urban centers
  • C
    Forcing families to move into cities
  • D
    Restricting audio dramas to local channels
💡 B — "connecting remote, rural communities with major urban centers"

تدريب — القطعة الرابعة: Roller Coasters

⏱ ص ٣٩-٤١▼
Roller coasters are popular amusement park rides designed to induce feelings of excitement, thrill, and controlled fear. Although they may appear dangerous, they are exceptionally well-engineered machines built under strict regulatory safety standards. The intense thrill stems from rapid changes in velocity and the physical effects of gravity on the passenger's body. The ride relies on potential energy accumulated as the train is pulled up the first hill. Once the train crosses the highest peak, this energy is converted into kinetic energy as gravity drops the train downward at breathtaking speeds.
Q1: What is the primary purpose behind the design of a roller coaster?
  • A
    To provide modern urban transportation
  • B
    To generate intense excitement and fear under safe conditions
  • C
    To test high-speed trains in bad weather
  • D
    To prove that gravity does not affect machines
💡 B — "designed to induce feelings of excitement, thrill, and controlled fear"
Q2: The word "strict" is closest in meaning to:
  • A
    Loose or optional
  • B
    Rigid and exact
  • C
    Outdated and old
  • D
    Cheap and simple
💡 B — strict = صارم / دقيق

٢. المقارنة والتفضيل

قاعدة المقارنة
مقارنة بين شيئين — صفة قصيرة

حوار ١ — في المستشفى (Doctor & Patient)

⏱ تدريب▼

🎧 الحوار — اضغط تشغيل واستمع

🎧 اضغط تشغيل لسماع الحوار بصوت بشري حقيقي
👨 Doctor Good morning! What seems to be the problem today?
👩 Patient I've had a terrible headache and a high fever since yesterday evening. I also feel very tired.
👨 Doctor I see. Let me check your temperature first. Do you have any allergies to medication?
👩 Patient No, I don't think so. Is it something serious?
👨 Doctor It's likely the flu. I'll prescribe some medicine. Take it twice a day. Make sure you rest and drink plenty of fluids.
